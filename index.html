Syllabus

The purpose of this course is to give you hands-on experience creating websites with interactive maps combining different types of spatial data. The course introduces the technology behind the maps—geographic information systems, server software, web services, and web development languages. The course will raise an awareness of these technologies’ capabilities and limitations, to build your capacity for further learning and decision-making in fields that produce or consume web-based maps.

By the end of this course, you should be able to create a website displaying interactive maps driven by geographic information, and apply your knowledge in academic and professional fields.

Topics

  • Geographic information and GIS
  • Web services and APIs
  • Web GIS infrastructure and cloud computing
  • Data formats and standards
  • Coordinate systems and cartography
  • Mobile GIS
  • Map tiles
  • Imagery
  • Geoprocessing
  • JavaScript and Google Earth Engine
  • Basics of webpage, HTML, & CSS
  • JavaScript web maps with Leaflet

Prerequisites

While there are no prerequisite courses, you must be computer literate to take this course. You should be familiar with common tasks such as using a web browser; signing up for online accounts; downloading, opening, zipping, renaming, copying, and organizing files and folders; installing software; editing documents and spreadsheets with a text editor or Excel; and navigating to files in Windows.

You are also required to have access to a computer, either your own computer or in a campus computer lab, such as the Geography and Anthropology computer lab in Howe-Russell E220.

Experience with GIS and programming is an advantage, but is not assumed.

Evaluation

Assignments 70%
Quizzes 10%
Final project 20%

Assignments

The ten required assignments are based on lessons but also introduce new material and skills. They include tasks such as building maps in ArcGIS Online and creating webpages. There are eleven assignments; you choose which ten to complete.

Quizzes

Two quizzes will be given based on lecture material to test knowledge of terminology and concepts.

Final project

The final project will be an opportunity to employ the concepts taught in the course in a website. The website will offer maps and other media to present information to your audience. The application must meet the core requirements defined in the project guidelines, but you will choose the best technologies and methods to deliver a satisfactory product.

Late submissions

An assignment not submitted by the deadline will lose a letter grade, and 5 points (out of 100) for each additional 24-hour period that the assignment is delinquent. Assignments, exams, and projects not completed by grade submission deadlines (midterm and final grades) must be graded as a zero. Assignments should be turned in as early as possible in case of problems with your Internet connection or computer close to the deadline.

Expectations of time

Whether online or in person, the content of this course is designed for about three hours per week of lecture or lesson, with an equal amount of time spent on the corresponding assignments.
